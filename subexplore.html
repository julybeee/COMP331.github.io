BACOLOD CITY

PIAYA


Piaya or piyaya is a well-known delicacy in this place because of its sweet filling available in different flavors but in recent years it has also seen in some exciting transformations. Its taste is one of a kind that strikes the perfect balance between savory and sweet.

REFERENCE IMAGE: https://www.foxyfolksy.com/piaya/

CHICKEN INASAL


Food adventure in Negros is not complete without chicken inasal. This favorite dish is especially popular in Bacolod flavored with vinegar , citrus juice, lemongrass, and achiote, and grilled to perfection. It is surely a guaranteed crowd-pleaser!

REFERENCE IMAGE:https://www.today.com/recipes/chicken-inasal-recipe-t219149

NAPOLEONES

This mouthwatering Bacolod’s Napoleones is what you need when you have a sweet tooth. Since the city of Bacolod, in the Philippines, is well known for its sugar industry. It is also famous for its sweet delicacies. Especialy this desert type pastry is an all-time favorite of locals and tourists because of its delicious, custard- like filling topped with sugar glaze which gives a light crunch once you take a bite. You will surely enjoy this treat!
REFERENCE IMAGE:https://cmsbcd.com/napoleones/

HINIGARAN

POTATO CAKE

It is a layered cake with a buttercream icing and a crunchy caramel topping. If eaten without the topping, it is mildly flavored with bits of potatoes. The topping add more flavor and texture to the cake. It’s sweet, buttery and the slightly caramelized sugar is crunchy to bite with the texture of sugar crystals. It is very tasty and sweet at the same time. The cake itself is moist and soft and it is pretty good. REFERENCE IMAGE:
https://www.flickr.com/photos/eazy360/19746363635

BITCHOKOY

This a like the local version of a doughnut but sprinkled with white sugar instead of glazed. It is a treat that brings back nostalgic memories and satisfies one's craving with a sweet and delightful bite. It is crispy, golden, soft and pillowy inside, these twisted fried donuts sprinkled with sugar. With its mouthwatering aroma to the crunchy outer layer down to the soft and fluffy bread inside!
REFERENCE IMAGE:
https://www.flickr.com/photos/56765035@N04/5921620357

TALABA

Are found in almost all coastal towns of Negros Occidental. Oysters are pretty standard and are essentially a popular seafood dish. It is best had with butter and lemon to enjoy the texture and taste of authentic oysters. They could have hints of flavors similar to beluga caviar or salmon. Also when taken together with a glass of wine or a cold mug of beer, these seashells are a great accompaniment. REFERENCE IMAGE:
http://islandofnegros.blogspot.com/2015/08/hinigarans-best-oyster.html?m=1

MANAPLA

PUTO


Fluffy, and tasty, this Filipino steamed rice cake makes a filling snack or a delicious side for savory dishes. Because of their neutral flavor, they can be enjoyed as a quick snack or a dessert, but they are also served as an accompaniment to savory dishes such as hearty Filipino stews.
REFERENCE IMAGE: https://hicaps.com.ph/puto-recipe-with-cheese/

SAN ENRIQUE

KAKANIN


This place is also famous for its kakanin. You will see along the highway of the place various stores selling delicacies like Alupi, Suman Latik, Bayi-Bayi, and much more. Its kakanin will entice you to rediscover our heritage through local kitchens, pasalubong on wheels and afternoon delights.
REFERENCE IMAGE:https://www.panlasangpinoyrecipes.com/the-big-four-top-loved-kakanin-and-their-origins/

BUKO PIE


It is an ultimate baked treat! With tender young coconut meat in a creamy filling and a crisp, buttery pastry crust, this Filipino coconut pie is perfect for a snack or dessert!

REFERENCE IMAGE: https://www.createwithnestle.ph/recipes/creamy-buco-pie

ALUPI


Cassava Suman or Alupi is a great snack anytime of the day, made with grated cassava, sugar and young coconut wrapped in banana leaves and steamed. This kakanin is then wrapped in banana leaves and steamed then its ready to be eaten as an all-around snack.

REFERENCE IMAGE: https://www.angsarap.net/2018/10/26/cassava-suman-with-macapuno/

SUMAN LATIK


Suman Malagkit especially with Coconut Caramel Sauce steamed in banana leaves is the perfect snack or dessert. This Filipino sticky rice is soft, chewy, and made extra delicious with a rich and creamy sauce. It's seriously addictive!

REFERENCE IMAGE: https://beingjellybeans.com/tag/suman-sa-latik/

BAYE BAYE


Baye-Baye is an Ilonggo favorite native snack usually made with ground toasted rice aka pinipig, coconut meat and white sugar. It is also known to be a perfect match for coffee or sodas. It is also well-loved by children because of its sweet consistent taste.

REFERENCE IMAGE: https://www.tasteatlas.com/baye-baye

SILAY CITY

PILI NUTS SQUARE


It’s like a paper-thin crust, which is a fabulous thing. It gives the pastry that sophisticated crackle with every bite. The rest of the pili square is super chewy (you really have to get your teeth into it) and surprisingly moist. And it is not too sweet. It is so easy to take bite after bite of it without noticing you've already consumed half of the box in under an hour.
REFERENCE IMAGE: https://www.angsarap.net/tag/nut/

EMPANADA


These delicious handheld pies are pastries stuffed with a variety of savoury fillings including beef, chicken, pork, cheese and vegetables. There’s also sweet versions too! They can be served as a snack or appetizer, but they are also great as a hearty lunch or dinner, guaranteed to keep you full and satisfied.
REFERENCE IMAGE: https://www.taste.com.au/recipes/cheats-chicken-enchilada-empanadas-recipe/dnd21av0

LUMPIA UBOD

These are fresh Filipino spring rolls made extra special with sautéed palm heart filling, homemade crepe wrappers, and a tasty garlic-peanut sauce. It comes with many varieties like sweet and garlicky gravy, while others are fried with spicy vinegar on the side for dipping. If you’re looking for a hearty and delicious midday snack or main dish, this is absolutely what you need.
REFERENCE IMAGE: https://justapieceofme.wordpress.com/tag/emma-lacson/

GUAPPLE PIE

As what is its name about, guapple pie is made from the guapple fruit (oversized guava). It is a perfect treat if you’re finding for a new way to satisfy your sweet tooth having the right amount of sweetness and crumby texture. It is also a favorite among the locals and a must-taste for all visitors. No surprise it constantly makes the lists of yummy desserts across the Philippines.
REFERENCE IMAGE: https://www.ilovetansyong.com/2016/02/review-guapple-pie-in-el-ideal.html?m=1

VICTORIAS CITY

BOTTLED BANGUS IN OLIVE OIL


Spanish-style Sardines Bangus in Oil is easy to make in the pressure cooker and so delicious with steamed rice! And great for gift-giving, too! This Filipino bangus in bottle is perfect when you want something easy to eat but yummy and nostalgic.
